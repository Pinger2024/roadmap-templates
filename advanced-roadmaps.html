Advanced planning

What is advanced planning?

Advanced planning helps teams turn a backlog into a realistic, time-based plan: mapping work to a timeline, balancing capacity, and keeping dependencies visible as priorities change.

Plan across teams

Bring multiple teams' work into a single view so you can see the whole programme rather than one board at a time. Group work by initiative, team, or release to understand where effort is concentrated and where the gaps are.

Visualise your roadmap

A timeline view shows when work is expected to start and finish, with milestones and releases marked along the way. Use it to spot clashes early and to communicate a plan to stakeholders.

  • Map epics and stories to a calendar to see the shape of delivery.
  • Mark releases and key dates as milestones.
  • Roll progress up from child issues so the view reflects reality.

Manage capacity and dependencies

Set how much each team can take on per sprint and let the plan flag when you have committed beyond that. Dependency links make it clear when one piece of work is waiting on another.

Tip: keep your plan honest by reviewing it regularly. Use the search above to jump straight to a section.

Scenario planning

Try out changes safely before committing them. Model what happens if a date moves, a team's capacity changes, or scope is added, then compare against your current plan before deciding.
